## Risk factors for 30-day mortality following major neonatal surgery: insights from a 10-year cohort in southern Tunisia.

- 来源: PubMed · 推送 01:48
- DOI: 10.1007/s00595-026-03233-z
- 获取全文: [DOI 全文](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00595-026-03233-z) · [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41553398/)

**中文总结**：- 研究目的：识别新生儿重大胸腹部手术后30天内死亡的危险因素。
- 核心发现/数据：该研究纳入361名新生儿，术后30天死亡率为23.5%（85例）。多变量逻辑回归分析显示，院外出生、早产、先天性心脏病、5分钟Apgar评分低及手术时间延长是术后30天死亡的独立危险因素。
- 临床启示：这些危险因素可用于识别高风险新生儿，并指导临床优化围手术期管理，以降低术后死亡率。

**原文摘要**：This study aimed to identify the risk factors for 30-day mortality following neonatal surgery for major thoracic and abdominal conditions. We conducted a retrospective cohort study of neonates who underwent major noncardiac abdominal or thoracic surgery in a tertiary pediatric surgery center in Tunisia between April 2015 and March 2025. A total of 361 neonates underwent major abdominal or thoracic surgeries during the study period. The male-to-female ratio was 1.3:1. The most common surgical conditions were esophageal atresia (n = 105), duodenal atresia (n = 42), and anorectal malformation (n = 39). A total of 85 neonates (23.5%) died within 30 days of surgery. According to a multivariate logistic regression analysis, five variables were independently associated with 30-day mortality. This study demonstrated that an outborn status, prematurity, congenital heart disease, low 5-minute Apgar score, and prolonged operative time predict 30-day mortality after major neonatal surgery. These factors could serve as valuable tools for identifying patients at increased risk and enhancing the quality of their management.
