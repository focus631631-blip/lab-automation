Risk factors for 30-day mortality following major neonatal surgery: insights from a 10-year cohort in southern Tunisia.

PubMed · DOI 全文 · PubMed
- 研究目的：识别新生儿重大胸腹部手术后30天死亡率的独立风险因素。
- 核心发现/数据：在突尼斯南部一家三级儿科手术中心2015年4月至2025年3月的回顾性队列中，共纳入361名接受重大非心脏胸腹部手术的新生儿，术后30天死亡率为23.5%（85例）。多因素Logistic回归分析显示，院外出生、早产、先天性心脏病、5分钟Apgar评分低和手术时间长是术后30天死亡的独立风险因素。
- 临床启示：这些风险因素可用于早期识别高危新生儿，从而优化围手术期管理并改善预后。
原文摘要

This study aimed to identify the risk factors for 30-day mortality following neonatal surgery for major thoracic and abdominal conditions. We conducted a retrospective cohort study of neonates who underwent major noncardiac abdominal or thoracic surgery in a tertiary pediatric surgery center in Tunisia between April 2015 and March 2025. A total of 361 neonates underwent major abdominal or thoracic surgeries during the study period. The male-to-female ratio was 1.3:1. The most common surgical conditions were esophageal atresia (n = 105), duodenal atresia (n = 42), and anorectal malformation (n = 39). A total of 85 neonates (23.5%) died within 30 days of surgery. According to a multivariate logistic regression analysis, five variables were independently associated with 30-day mortality. This study demonstrated that an outborn status, prematurity, congenital heart disease, low 5-minute Apgar score, and prolonged operative time predict 30-day mortality after major neonatal surgery. These factors could serve as valuable tools for identifying patients at increased risk and enhancing the quality of their management.
